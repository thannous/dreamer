<h1 class="font-serif text-4xl md:text-5xl mb-4 text-dream-cream">Dream content and dataset methodology</h1>
<p class="text-sm text-purple-300/70 mb-6">Published and last checked July 9, 2026</p>
<p class="text-lg text-purple-200/80 leading-relaxed mb-10">This report separates Noctalia's auditable public editorial catalog from private dream journals and from research datasets. It states what can be verified in the current source, how that content is structured and which claims should not be made from it.</p>

<div class="space-y-8 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
  <section class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-dream-salmon/20 bg-dream-salmon/5">
    <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">Status of the “55,000 dreams” figure</h2>
    <p>Noctalia's current public source does not contain 55,000 dream records, a dated aggregate export or the provenance needed to reproduce that number. It also contains no documented sampling frame, consent process, annotation protocol or statistical output for such a corpus. The figure is therefore <strong class="text-white">not independently auditable from the current evidence and must not be cited as research support</strong>.</p>
    <p class="mt-4">Private journal entries are deliberately not published here. If a future aggregate analysis is released, it should include a collection window, inclusion criteria, privacy treatment, deduplication rules, sample size calculation, annotation method and reproducible results before any headline number is used.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-white/5">
    <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">What is verifiable in the public source</h2>
    <dl class="grid gap-5 md:grid-cols-3">
      <div><dt class="text-sm text-purple-200/70">Dream-symbol records</dt><dd class="text-2xl text-white font-serif">150</dd><p class="text-sm mt-1">One versioned editorial record per symbol.</p></div>
      <div><dt class="text-sm text-purple-200/70">Languages</dt><dd class="text-2xl text-white font-serif">5</dd><p class="text-sm mt-1">English, French, Spanish, German and Italian fields.</p></div>
      <div><dt class="text-sm text-purple-200/70">Blog content sources</dt><dd class="text-2xl text-white font-serif">235</dd><p class="text-sm mt-1">44 BlogPosting families + 3 thematic hubs = 235 localized files.</p></div>
    </dl>
    <p class="mt-6">The 150 base symbol records have extended editorial copy across the same 150 identifiers. These counts describe published content objects, not unique dreamers, user submissions or experimental observations.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-white/5">
    <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">How the symbol catalog is organized</h2>
    <p>Each base record has a stable identifier, category and localized fields such as name, URL slug, short description, reflection questions and FAQ copy. Longer interpretations and variations are maintained separately but keyed to the same identifiers. The site generator turns those records into localized public pages and checks language and image parity.</p>
    <p class="mt-4">The editorial purpose is to offer possible associations and questions. The content does not assign a universal meaning, clinical label or predicted outcome to a symbol.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">
    <div class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-white/5">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">Privacy boundary</h2>
      <p>The public catalog and articles do not contain private user journal entries. App data handling is documented separately in the <a href="/en/privacy-policy" class="text-dream-salmon hover:underline">privacy policy</a>. Public content counts must never be presented as counts of people or private dreams.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-white/5">
      <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">Editorial review boundary</h2>
      <p>Thanh Chau, a software engineer and long-time dream journaler, leads the product and editorial process. Noctalia does not name a clinician or academic researcher as reviewer of this methodology. Content is informational and should not replace qualified care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-white/5">
    <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">How to cite this material accurately</h2>
    <ul class="list-disc pl-5 space-y-2">
      <li>Describe it as the Noctalia public editorial dream-symbol catalog.</li>
      <li>Include the page URL and the July 9, 2026 snapshot date when quoting counts.</li>
      <li>Do not describe the catalog as a clinical study, representative survey or dataset of user dreams.</li>
      <li>For scientific claims, follow the primary studies linked from individual articles rather than citing Noctalia as the original research source.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="glass-panel p-8 rounded-2xl border border-white/10 bg-white/5">
    <h2 class="text-2xl font-serif text-white mb-4">Methodology FAQ</h2>
    <div class="space-y-5">
      <div><h3 class="text-white font-medium">Does Noctalia publish an auditable dataset of 55,000 user dreams?</h3><p class="mt-1">No. The current public source does not contain 55,000 dream records or the provenance needed to reproduce that figure. Noctalia therefore does not treat it as verified research evidence.</p></div>
      <div><h3 class="text-white font-medium">What public scope is verifiable today?</h3><p class="mt-1">As of July 9, 2026, the source contains 150 editorial dream-symbol records in five languages and 44 BlogPosting families plus 3 thematic hubs, equal to 235 localized files.</p></div>
      <div><h3 class="text-white font-medium">Are private journal entries used in the public dictionary?</h3><p class="mt-1">No private user journal entries are included in the public content files. The public symbol records and articles are editorial material.</p></div>
      <div><h3 class="text-white font-medium">Can the Noctalia catalog be cited as a clinical research dataset?</h3><p class="mt-1">No. It has no published participant cohort, sampling protocol, clinical labels or validation study. Cite it as an editorial catalog, not as clinical evidence.</p></div>
    </div>
